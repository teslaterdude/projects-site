Old Town / Central Scottsdale

Scottsdale — Old Town

Good Budget Fit
$700K – $1.1M
4–5 bed, 2,500–3,500 sq ft

Old Town Scottsdale hits the lifestyle trifecta: walkable streets lined with restaurants and galleries, world-class golf right in your backyard, and Camelback Mountain 15 minutes away for the mornings when you need a great hike. It's the most "alive" neighborhood in the metro — the kind of place where you can walk to dinner on a Tuesday in November. For a couple coming from downtown Milwaukee who likes energy and convenience, this is the closest analog.

Arcadia

Budget Stretch (Upper Range)
$750K – $1.1M
4–5 bed, 2,500–3,500 sq ft (Arcadia Lite: $650–850K)

❄️ Snowbird / Semi-Permanent Resident Considerations

Things that matter specifically for a Nov–May lifestyle, that most relocation guides skip

Arizona Property Taxes Are Refreshingly Low

AZ's effective property tax rate is ~0.5–0.7% vs. Wisconsin's 1.5–2%. On an $800K home, expect ~$4,000–5,600/year in property taxes vs. $12,000–16,000 for a comparable Milwaukee property. This meaningfully improves your carrying cost math.

HOA Rules on Short-Term Rentals

Since you won't be there May–October, renting the home during summer could generate $3,000–5,000/month in short-term rental income. However, many HOAs in planned communities restrict this — research HOA rules carefully in any home you're serious about. Older/non-HOA areas like Arcadia and Old Town give you more flexibility.

Pool Maintenance Remotely Is Very Manageable

Monthly pool service in Phoenix runs $80–150/month and companies are plentiful. You can have your home "watched" professionally while in Wisconsin for relatively little cost. Many semi-permanent residents do this with no issues.

Anne's Derm PA Job Market

Scottsdale and North Phoenix/Paradise Valley have the most concentrated dermatology practices, reflecting the affluent snowbird-heavy patient population that drives demand for cosmetic derm. If Anne's practice location matters, areas like Old Town Scottsdale, Arcadia, and North Scottsdale put her closest to the highest-density derm clinic clusters.

Establish AZ as Your Primary Residence for Tax Benefits

Since Wisconsin has higher income taxes, switching your primary domicile to Arizona (lower flat state income tax of 2.5% as of 2023) could have meaningful benefits given your income. Worth discussing with a CPA, especially as you manage the Milwaukee rental portfolio remotely.

Budget for the First Summer Away

Even with professional management, first-year Phoenix homeowners often have a list of things to fix or update after the summer heat. Phoenix summers are extreme (~115°F peaks) — budget $3,000–5,000 for first-year carrying/maintenance costs as a buffer, especially for any home with an older HVAC system.

🗺️ April Tour Itinerary — What to Do in Each Area

April is perfect timing — the weather is excellent (75–90°F), the snowbird crowds are thinning, and you'll see the neighborhoods at their most authentic. Here's what to specifically look for and do in each area.

Old Town Scottsdale

Walk 5th Avenue and Scottsdale Road. Have lunch at Culinary Dropout or Postino. Walk through the arts district. Drive through the residential streets east of Scottsdale Road between Indian School and Camelback — this is where the housing stock for your budget lives. Morning: hike Camelback's Echo Canyon trail (go early — busy by 8am in April).

Arcadia

Drive Thomas Road between 40th St and 68th St — this is the heart of Arcadia's restaurant corridor. Have brunch at Ingo's or Postino Arcadia. Walk through the residential streets south of Camelback — you'll feel the neighborhood's charm immediately. Note: canal path along the Arizona Canal is great for a walk with the stroller.

Ahwatukee Foothills

Drive Chandler Blvd through the neighborhood, then head to South Mountain Park. Hike the Telegraph Pass Trail (easy-moderate, stunning views). Stop by Foothills Golf Club and ask to tour. Drive the streets backing up to the mountain preserve — these are the premium lots. April temps are perfect for hiking here (vs. impossible in summer).

Gilbert

Start with breakfast at Joe's Farm Grill in Agritopia — you'll understand the community's character in 30 minutes. Walk through the Heritage District for lunch. Hit the Riparian Preserve at Water Ranch for a casual nature walk — great with a baby stroller. Drive through the Spectrum subdivision for an example of newer construction at your price point.

Chandler

Downtown Chandler is best experienced at dinner — try The Brickyard. Walk the downtown square during the day. If you can, ask a local realtor to show you homes in the north Chandler area (closer to Tempe border) and in south Chandler near the Warner/Germann area — two different vibes worth comparing.

Cave Creek / Carefree

Take Cave Creek Road north from Scottsdale. Stop in downtown Cave Creek for a drink at the Roadhouse. Continue to Carefree's town center — walk around, go into a gallery. Drive Spur Cross Road toward Spur Cross Ranch. Even a short drive shows why this area is special. Allow 45 min each way from Old Town Scottsdale.

Fountain Hills

Drive the Beeline Highway from Scottsdale — the approach into town is stunning. Go to Fountain Park and see the fountain in action (runs at the top of each hour). Walk the lakeside path. Have lunch downtown. Drive the ridgeline streets above town — the mountain views from these homes are dramatic. Note how it feels relative to other areas in terms of isolation.

North Scottsdale (McCormick Ranch)

Drive the Indian Bend Greenbelt — this tells you everything about the lifestyle here. Stop at McCormick Ranch Golf Club and ask to walk the course or have lunch at the clubhouse. Drive through DC Ranch to compare the luxury tier. Pricing context: note what $800–900K gets you here vs. what it gets you in Chandler or Ahwatukee.
